Thomas Lehmann

Edmonton, AB · Canada · (780) 604-2794 · thomasl86@gmail.com

I am experienced in and passionate about the fields of mechatronics and robotics research. In recent years, the focus of my research has been on developing novel technologies for robotic assistance during the minimally invasive medical procedure prostate brachytherapy. During this research, I have published original research on the estimation of needle deflection, trajectory prediction, and developed and published a novel method for the steering of flexible needles under the supervision of Dr. Mahdi Tavakoli of the Telerobotic and Biorobotic Systems Laboratory at the University of Alberta.

Most recently, I have joined the Additive Design and Manufacturing Systems Laboratory at the University of Alberta led by Dr. Ahmed Qureshi in collaboration with InnoTech Alberta as a Postdoctoral Researcher. Currently my main area of research is robotic large-scale metal additive manufacturing where the kinematic flexibility of a 6-axis robotic articulated arm combined with a 2-axis positioner are leveraged for support-free large-scale printing of metal parts using wire-arc welding or laser-based direct energy deposition technology. As a new and innovative technology, robotic additive manufacturing is a very exciting and rewarding research project to be involved in with potential to significantly contribute to the state of the art and to advance manufacturing methodologies.
